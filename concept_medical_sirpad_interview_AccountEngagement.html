Inside SirPAD: the world’s largest randomized trial of a sirolimus coated balloon in PAD

Featuring the SirPAD investigators
Prof. Dr. med. Nils Kucher & Prof. Dr. med. Stefano Barco — University Hospital Zurich
In this interview, the principal investigators discuss the primary results of SirPAD — an investigator-led, all-comers randomized controlled trial evaluating the MagicTouch PTA Sirolimus Coated Balloon against uncoated balloon angioplasty for peripheral artery disease. Presented as a Late-Breaking Clinical Trial at ACC 2026 and published simultaneously in the New England Journal of Medicine, SirPAD enrolled 1,252 patients with femoropopliteal or below-the-knee PAD.
Primary Endpoint — Major Adverse Limb Events at 1 Year

Angioplasty
Median unbiased risk difference of −4.9% — both non-inferior and superior in favour of MagicTouch PTA.
The trial also showed a statistically significant reduction in the composite key secondary endpoint of any unplanned target-limb amputation or target-limb revascularization — in a true all-comers population, with nearly half of patients presenting with acute or chronic limb-threatening ischemia.

Trial Snapshot
Device MagicTouch PTA Sirolimus Coated Balloon
Design Investigator-initiated, multicentre, randomized, open-label, all-comers RCT
Population 1,252 patients with femoropopliteal or below-the-knee PAD
Comparison MagicTouch PTA vs. any uncoated balloon angioplasty (POBA)
Presented / Published ACC 2026 Scientific Sessions • New England Journal of Medicine
Investigators Prof. Dr. med. Nils Kucher & Prof. Dr. med. Stefano Barco, University Hospital Zurich
